Golden Egg Fund – Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Policy
(Internal Use Only)
Last updated: 12-23-25
This Anti-Money Laundering (“AML”) Policy outlines Golden Egg Fund’s approach to preventing money laundering, terrorist financing, and other illicit financial activity. This document is maintained for internal, compliance, and partner review purposes only.
________________________________________
1. Purpose
Golden Egg Fund is committed to complying with applicable anti-money laundering laws and regulations. The purpose of this policy is to describe how Golden Egg Fund mitigates financial crime risk through reliance on regulated third-party service providers.
________________________________________
2. Business Model Overview
Golden Egg Fund operates as a technology platform that enables users to access financial services through regulated third-party providers.
•	Golden Egg Fund does not custody customer funds or securities
•	Golden Egg Fund does not execute trades directly
•	All brokerage, clearing, custody, and settlement services are provided by a regulated third-party broker-dealer
•	Bank account verification is performed through Plaid
________________________________________
3. AML & KYC Responsibility
Golden Egg Fund relies on its regulated broker-dealer partner to perform:
•	Customer identification (KYC)
•	Identity verification
•	Sanctions screening
•	AML monitoring
•	Suspicious activity reporting (SARs)
Golden Egg Fund does not collect or store sensitive identity documents such as Social Security numbers or government-issued identification.
________________________________________
4. Transaction Monitoring
Transaction monitoring, including detection of suspicious or unusual activity, is performed by the broker-dealer and payment partners in accordance with applicable regulations.
Golden Egg Fund may assist by:
•	Cooperating with partner investigations
•	Providing relevant platform usage information when legally requested
________________________________________
5. Reporting & Cooperation
Golden Egg Fund cooperates fully with lawful requests from regulators, financial institutions, and law enforcement agencies as required by applicable law.
________________________________________
6. Employee Responsibilities
Access to internal systems and data is restricted based on role and necessity. Golden Egg Fund personnel are expected to adhere to internal security and data-handling standards.
________________________________________
7. Policy Review
This AML Policy is reviewed periodically and updated as necessary to reflect changes in business operations, regulatory requirements, or partner obligations.
